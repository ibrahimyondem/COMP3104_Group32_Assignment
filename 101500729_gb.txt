George Brown College is one of the major, and most famous, colleges of Toronto, ON, Canada.
It was founded 58 years ago, and as of today, it offers more than 170 full-time programs in art and design, 
business, community services, early childhood education, construction and engineering technologies, 
health sciences, hospitality and culinary arts, preparatory studies, 
as well as specialized programs and services for recent immigrants and international students.

Casa Loma campus, where my program (T177 - Computer Programming and Analysis) is being taught, is situated on the stretch of Kendal Ave. between Davenport Rd. and MacPherson Ave. 
Nearby features include Casa Loma, and the City of Toronto Archives. The campus itself is a collection of five buildings.

George Brown College provides a blend of theory and hands-on experience. 
It prioritizes real-world learning and provides students with opportunities to work alongside industry 
professionals in applied research and field education opportunities. And their industry partnerships 
mean students get the latest trends and tech they can expect in the field.
